## PART I — MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE

### 1. Purpose

The parties are engaged in a business relationship involving software development, product strategy, and related activities (the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, each party may disclose to the other certain confidential and proprietary information. This Agreement governs the protection of that information.

### 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by one party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that is designated confidential or reasonably understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- technical data, source code, and product roadmaps
- business strategies, financial data, customer lists, and pricing information
- personnel information, compensation structures, and equity arrangements
- research, competitive analyses, and product specifications
- the existence and terms of this Agreement

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure
(c) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
(d) must be disclosed pursuant to law or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order

### 3. Obligations

Each Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence using reasonable care
(b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without written consent
(c) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose
(d) restrict disclosure to persons who have a legitimate need to know

### 4. Term

Confidentiality obligations remain in effect for three (3) years from the Effective Date. Trade secrets remain protected indefinitely until no longer qualifying as trade secrets under applicable law.

### 5. Return or Destruction of Information

Upon request or termination of the relationship, the Receiving Party will promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information and certify in writing that it has done so.

### 6. Remedies

Each party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm. The injured party may seek injunctive relief in addition to any other remedies available at law.

## PART II — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

### 7. Work Product

"Work Product" means all inventions, discoveries, developments, improvements, software, code, algorithms, architectures, designs, works of authorship, trade secrets, processes, formulas, data, and other intellectual property created by Founder during their engagement with the Company that:

(a) relates to the Company's business
(b) uses Company resources
(c) results from work performed for the Company

### 8. Assignment of Intellectual Property

Founder hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all worldwide right, title, and interest in all Work Product, including associated patents, copyrights, trade secrets, trademarks, and other intellectual property rights.

To the extent any Work Product qualifies as a "work made for hire," it shall be owned by the Company. Otherwise, this Agreement operates as a present, automatic assignment of ownership.

### 9. Prior Inventions

Founder represents that no prior inventions, software, or intellectual property exist that are excluded from this Agreement unless disclosed in Exhibit B before signing.

If any prior invention is incorporated into Company Work Product, the Company is granted a perpetual, royalty-free license to use that invention.

### 10. Cooperation

Founder agrees to execute documents necessary to perfect the Company's ownership of Work Product.

If Founder cannot be located after reasonable effort, the Company is appointed attorney-in-fact solely for executing documents required to secure IP ownership.

### 11. Moral Rights

Founder waives any moral rights in the Work Product to the extent permitted by law.

## PART III — COMPANY CAPITALIZATION

### 12. Authorized Shares

The Company is authorized to issue 10,000,000 shares of common stock.

Founder ownership percentages described in this Agreement represent ownership on a fully diluted basis, including future equity issued to employees, advisors, or investors.

### 13. Founder Equity and Roles

Initial ownership of the Company is allocated as follows:

**Timothy Nguyen — Founder / Chief Executive Officer (35%)**
- Equity Ownership: 35% of fully diluted shares
- Responsibilities: hiring and talent acquisition, product strategy, PRD creation, company strategy, financing, and final authority on all non-technical matters

**Ved Sharma — Co-Founder / Chief Technology Officer (30%)**
- Equity Ownership: 30% of fully diluted shares
- Responsibilities: architectural decisions, technical direction, technical evaluation of engineering candidates, and final authority on all technical implementation matters

**Aryan Pandit — Co-Founder (15%)**
- Equity Ownership: 15% of fully diluted shares
- Responsibilities: routing and ranking systems, backend infrastructure

In the event of a disagreement between the CEO and CTO on a technical matter, the CTO shall have final authority on implementation decisions. On matters of company strategy, hiring, finance, and product direction, the CEO shall have final authority.

### 14. Employee Equity Pool

The Company reserves 20% of fully diluted shares for future grants to employees, advisors, and contractors through an employee equity incentive plan. These shares are unissued and held in the Company's option pool.

### 14.5 Employee Equity Pool Adjustments

Any increase to the employee equity pool beyond 20% of fully diluted shares requires the written consent of all founders then in Continuous Service. Unused shares in the employee equity pool remain the property of the Company and may be reallocated by the Board within the approved pool size.

## PART IV — VESTING

### 15. Vesting Schedule

Founder shares vest over four (4) years as follows:

- 25% vest after one year (cliff)
- Remaining 75% vest monthly over the following 36 months

Founder must remain in Continuous Service to receive vesting.

"Continuous Service" means continued service as a founder, employee, officer, or contractor of the Company without interruption.

### 15.5 Founder Commitment

Each founder agrees to devote reasonable professional effort to advancing the Company's objectives. Disagreements about the level of contribution shall be resolved through good-faith discussion among the founders before any action is taken under this Agreement.

### 16. Acceleration on Financing

If the Company closes a Qualified Financing of at least $500,000, six (6) months of vesting will accelerate for any founder still in Continuous Service at the time of closing.

### 17. Acceleration on Change of Control

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, all unvested shares immediately vest provided the founder remains in Continuous Service at closing.

### 18. Repurchase Rights

If a founder's Continuous Service terminates for any reason before full vesting, the Company may repurchase only the unvested shares at the original purchase price within ninety (90) days of termination. If not exercised within that period, the right lapses.

### 19. Definition of Cause

"Cause" means the occurrence of any of the following, as determined by a majority vote of the Board of Directors:

(a) Breach of confidentiality — unauthorized disclosure of Company information, code, IP, or business strategy to any third party
(b) Theft or misappropriation of Company intellectual property, code, data, or assets
(c) Gross negligence or reckless disregard in the performance of duties to the Company
(d) Indictment for or conviction of a felony
(e) Willful failure or refusal to comply with material Company policy after written notice

If termination occurs for Cause, the Company may repurchase only the unvested shares at the original purchase price. Vested shares are not subject to repurchase for any reason and remain the property of Founder.

## PART V — FOUNDER COMPENSATION

### 20. Compensation Upon Revenue Milestone

If the Company achieves monthly recurring revenue of at least $50,000 for two (2) consecutive months ("Revenue Milestone"), the Company shall allocate 20% of gross monthly revenue toward founder compensation, distributed among founders in proportion to their equity ownership. This entitlement is automatic upon achieving the Revenue Milestone and does not require Board approval. The Company shall notify all founders in writing within five (5) business days of achieving the Revenue Milestone.

### 20.5 Compensation Upon Qualified Financing

Upon closing of a Qualified Financing, founder compensation — including salary, bonuses, and any other forms of payment — shall be determined by the Board of Directors taking into account market compensation benchmarks and the Company's financial condition. Total founder compensation may not exceed 20% of the Company's annual US revenue without the written consent of all founders then in Continuous Service.

### 21. Pre-Milestone Compensation

Prior to a Qualified Financing or Revenue Milestone, founders may work without salary. Any pre-milestone compensation requires the written agreement of all founders then in Continuous Service.

## PART VI — GOVERNANCE AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 22. Board of Directors

The Company shall initially be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of one (1) director:

- Timothy Nguyen (Founder / CEO)

The Board may be expanded in connection with a Qualified Financing to include investor representatives and additional founders, as agreed at the time of financing. The expected post-financing structure is three (3) seats: CEO, one founder representative, and one investor representative.

### 22.5 Founder Removal Protection

A founder may not be removed from their role or terminated from Continuous Service without the approval of a majority vote of all founders then in Continuous Service, where majority means collectively holding more than 50% of the voting power among active founders. Removal of a founder shall not affect their vested equity. Vested shares remain the property of the founder regardless of the circumstances of departure.

### 23. Reserved Decisions

The following actions require the written approval of all founders then in Continuous Service, regardless of Board composition:

(a) Issuance of new shares or expansion of the employee equity pool beyond 20% of fully diluted shares
(b) Approval of a Qualified Financing or Change of Control
(c) Setting or materially reducing any founder's compensation below the floors in Section 20
(d) Any single expenditure exceeding $10,000 not previously budgeted

### 24. Future Equity Issuances

The Company may issue additional shares, options, or other securities for financing, employee compensation, or other corporate purposes, subject to the approval requirements in Section 23. All founders shall receive written notice of any proposed issuance at least ten (10) days in advance. Such issuances may dilute existing shareholders.

### 25. Information Rights

For so long as a founder holds at least 5% of the Company's fully diluted shares, the Company shall provide:

(a) Monthly unaudited financial statements within fifteen (15) days after month end
(b) Annual financial statements within sixty (60) days after fiscal year end
(c) Prompt written notice of any material event, including a Qualified Financing, Change of Control, or material litigation

### 26. 83(b) Election

Founders are responsible for filing an 83(b) election with the IRS within thirty (30) days of receiving shares. Failure to file may result in significant tax liability. The Company makes no representation regarding tax consequences. Each founder should consult a tax advisor.

### 27. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Georgia. Disputes shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Georgia.

### 28. Entire Agreement

This document constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements. Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

### 29. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
